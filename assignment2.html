Property Sales in Melbourne

Ever since the first Covid-19 lockdown of the city Wuhan in China, the world has been in disorder and turmoil. The first case in Australia was reported on the 25th January, 2020 in Melbourne. This visualisation will study how Covid-19 affected property sales in Melbourne.

Comparing Suburbs in Melbourne

Types of Property Sold

In this visualisation, the properties sold are categorised as houses, townhouses and units. The green and red region shows Pre-Covid and Post-Covid respectively. The overall properties sold dropped significantly after Covid-19.

Mean Prices of Properties

The green and red region shows Pre-Covid and Post-Covid respectively. When Covid-19 first hit Melbourne in 2020, prices of properties were high which was then followed by a dip before gradually rising back up in the second half of 2020. Although the trends were visually different, the overall mean price of properties were very similar during Pre-Covid and Post-Covid with a slight increase during the latter.

Comparing with other cities in Australia

Among the different cities, Melbourne had the greatest drop in total properties sold after Covid-19. Sydney, Brisbane and Perth saw an increase in properties sold within the same period. The mean price in all cities increased after Covid-19 with Melbourne having the least increase and Sydney having the greatest.
